Submission Guidelines

Papers should be formatted according to the CEUR-ART style formatting guidelines and submitted as a single PDF file.

We welcome submissions across the full spectrum of theoretical and practical work including research ideas, methods, tools, simulations, applications or demos, practical evaluations, and surveys.

  • Short papers (2 pages, excluding references and appendices): considered for a short presentation.
  • Regular papers (4–7 pages, excluding references and appendices): considered for a regular presentation.

All papers will be peer-reviewed in a single-blind process and assessed based on their novelty, technical quality, potential impact, clarity, and reproducibility (when applicable).

Extended abstracts of published papers are welcome, but they should demonstrate their close link to the topic of the workshop.
