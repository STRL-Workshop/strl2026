---
title: "STRL 2026"
---

As AI systems increasingly rely on foundation models, multimodal perception, and large-scale spatio-temporal data, they still struggle with robust generalization, causal understanding, and explainable decision-making in dynamic environments.

**STRL 2026** aims to strengthen the bridge between the **Knowledge Representation and Reasoning (KRR)** and **Machine Learning (ML)** communities by bringing together work on neuro-symbolic methods, commonsense reasoning, language and vision grounding, world models, benchmarks, and explainability.

Spatio-temporal reasoning is becoming a central bottleneck for modern AI — across language, vision, robotics, and multi-agent settings, current learning-based systems often fail when faced with distribution shifts, long-horizon dependencies, counterfactual queries, or the need to justify decisions in dynamic environments.

We argue that the calculi associated with the spatial and temporal reasoning community, be they qualitative or quantitative, naturally build upon physics and human cognition, and could therefore form a module that would be beneficial towards causal representation learning. A (symbolic) spatio-temporal knowledge base could provide a dependable causal seed upon which machine learning models could generalize, and exploring this direction from various perspectives is the main theme of this workshop.

The workshop will provide a focused forum for exchanging ideas and identifying shared research challenges and opportunities for building **dependable spatio-temporal AI**.
